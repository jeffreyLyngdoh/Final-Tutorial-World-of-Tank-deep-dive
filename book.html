Introduction

World of Tanks is perhaps one of the most data intensive games in the world. With more than seven hundred tanks and hundreds of thousands of players the game draws a very dedicated and competitive player base. Analyzing the patterns of this player base and vehicles I will attempt to highlight an unfair advantage that is rooted in the game as well as constructing a rudimentary machine learning algorithm which makes predictions based on this data.

I have played the game more than I would like to admit. I often spend a lot of time examining my own as well as other tanks and player statistics. There are websites devoted to keeping real time data on players. Tomato.gg is one of these websites which aggregate player and tank statics. After committing so much time to this game I decided it is important to obtain some tangible knowledge from it. The vast amounts of data that are publicly available can be applied to a data science pipeline. It is my intention to use this data combined with my own intuition about the game to map some of the broader trends within the game.

My own stats: https://www.tomato.gg/stats/NA/Jeffrey1470=1020690095

Aggregate tank stats: https://www.tomato.gg/tank-stats/NA

Over the years I have become quite acquainted with some of the issues that plague the game. A main complaint is the advantage paying players have over those who dont. The easiest way to examine this is to study the difference between premium and standard tanks. Premium tanks are tanks which can largely only be acquitted by microtransactions. These tanks have an advantage over standard ones,often given preferential matchmaking as well as being able to earn credits more efficiently compared to standard ones.

My goal is to use the data between Standard and premium tanks as a means of demonstrating the difference between the two in win rate and credit earning. I hope to use this data as well as other factors in order to make a function that can predict the expected amount of profit a person can make from a battle.

A brief TLDR World of Tanks is a massive multiplayer game in which players are pitted against each other in random battles. These battles may have different objectives ranging from capturing the other team's control point, attacking or defending a control point against another team, or destroying all enemy vehicles. Players may choose from a number of vehicle types; light tanks, medium tanks, heavy tanks, tank destroyers or self propelled guns. Each of these tanks have different roles you can read about them below.

https://worldoftanks.com/en/content/guide/general/vehicle_types/

Vehicles are grouped by nation and divided into 10 tiers. Each tier is basically a level of difficulty and experience with tier 1 being for beginners and tier 10 being the hardest for more experienced players.

Tanks made available through a tech tree. A player starting at tier 1 needs to earn experience and credits through battles.

Credits and experience is gained through battles. Winning a battle and contributing with damage and other factors will net you more credits and experiences. The game deducts a repair cost from your earnings if your vehicle is damaged or destroyed. In addition to ammo cost credits, you need to pay for every shell you fire. There are different types of shells which can range in price, you need to pick which ones you want to fire.

Note it is possible to lose credits during a battle if you repair and ammo costs exceed what you earn. This is more common in higher tiers like 8,9, and 10 where tanks have higher repair costs.

Once they have enough experience and credits they can research the tank at the next tier (unlock it) then purchase it with enough credits. Below is an example of a french tech tree.

https://tanks.gg/techtree/france

As you can see a player needs to follow a path and work one tank at a time up the tree to unlock the best tanks at the highest tier, there are a couple branches one can follow and class can change from one tier to another. For example the light tank line branches to a medium tank along the tree.

At the bottom you might have noticed a couple of vehicles that are not attached to the tree. These are premium tanks which are usually obtained through gold, and in-game currency you can only largely obtain through micro transactions. Premium tanks are available in every tier and do not require grinding. Purchasing a premium tank does not require experience, credits, or a certain amount of battles. You can purchase a premium without needing to unlock any of the tech tree tanks.

Note: Is possible to obtain premium tanks through special events or missions, though these are few and far betweens. Ive personally played the game for 7 years and only obtained two premiums in that time, both of which are lower tiers.

As it turns out premium tanks are able to earn greater profit than standard ones. They also have noticeably higher win rates. By analyzing the tank data from tomato gg I hope to demonstrate a correlation between being a premium tank and having an advantage in the game.

Exploratory data analysis and Hypothesis testing

Now that data is centralized let's start by observing some simple trends. Two features that are relatively initiative is winrate and profit. Having a higher winrate and gaining more profit in the game is the ultimate goal of each battle. We can observe a clear difference between standard free tanks and premium ones.

Let's start with winrate we can divide the data into two groups, premium and non premium dataframes. We can then place both on scatter plots. After that we can apply a line of best fit for both sets using a linear regression model.

The plot certainly seems to suggest a higher winrate for premium tanks compared to standard ones. However correlation is not always causation. Inorder to confidently make this conclusion we can perform hypothesis testing.

We can define our null and alternative hypothesis

Null hypothesis: The winrate of premium tanks is the same as the winrate of non-premium tanks.

Alternative hypothesis: The winrate of premium tanks is higher than the winrate of non-premium tanks.

We will set our significance level to 0.05 to reject the null hypothesis. We can then use scipy to calculate p value.

In [ ]:
gold_winrates = premiumGeneral['winrate']
standard_winrates = stdGeneral['winrate']

t_stat, p_val = stats.ttest_ind(gold_winrates, standard_winrates, equal_var=False)

print("t-statistic: " + str(t_stat))
print("p-value: " + str(p_val))

if(p_val < 0.05):
    print('Reject Null Hypothesis')
else:
    print('Null Hypotheis remains true')

We define a machine learning function using gradient descent. We will iterate T number of times each time we will keep track of the steepest ascent at f and step down a gradient. After we finish we will return our optimal theta values as well as the loss over the iterations.

In [ ]:
def grad_descent(X, y, T, alpha):
    m, n = X.shape 
    theta = np.zeros(n)
    f = np.zeros(T) 
    for i in range(T):

        f[i] = 0.5*np.linalg.norm(X.dot(theta) - y)**2

        g = np.transpose(X).dot(X.dot(theta) - y)

        theta = theta - alpha*g
    return theta, f

def predictProfit(isPrem, tier, survival, damage, repairCost, ammoCost, theta):
    profit = 0
    
    premNorm = (isPrem - np.mean(trainingData["binaryPrem"])) / np.std(trainingData["binaryPrem"])
    tierNorm = (tier - np.mean(trainingData["tier"])) / np.std(trainingData["tier"])
    survivalNorm = (survival - np.mean(trainingData['survival'])) / np.std(trainingData['survival'])
    damageNorm = (damage - np.mean(trainingData['damage'])) / np.std(trainingData['damage'])
    repairNorm = (repairCost - np.mean(trainingData['repairCost'])) / np.std(trainingData['repairCost'])
    ammoNorm = (ammoCost - np.mean(trainingData['avg_ammo_cost'])) / np.std(trainingData['avg_ammo_cost'])

    features = np.array([1, premNorm, tierNorm, survivalNorm, damageNorm, repairNorm, ammoNorm])
    profit = np.dot(features, theta)
    
    return profit

Curation message

Hopefully my work has provided a brief insight into some of the issues surrounding the world of tanks. We analyze the advantage premium tanks have over normal ones which is only a single factor in the larger issue of advantaging paying players over players who dont.

Now it is understandable that WarGaming is a company and they need to earn money however doing so at the expense of other players is not a fair or sustainable practice.

Newer players can become discouraged and frustrated by some of these blatant favoritism and it breeds a sense of resentment within the player base.

Winrates and credits ought to be based on skill and performance within a battle rather than whether a tank is paid for or not. I think an important chance Wargaming should make is really rewarding players based on consistency.

Making Premium tanks better than standard ones may be somewhat fair however in battles Wargaming should work to ensure an even number of premium tanks on both sides. This can make games more fair and even out the large difference in winrate between premium and non premium tanks.
